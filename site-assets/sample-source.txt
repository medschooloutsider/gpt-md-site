Study design

This is a case-control outbreak investigation. The appropriate measure of association is the odds ratio, not the risk ratio.

The exercise gives 26 cases and 26 controls from the same hotel population and asks for 2 x 2 tables for foods eaten at lunch.

Formula

OR = (a d) / (b c)

where:

- a = exposed cases
- b = unexposed cases
- c = exposed controls
- d = unexposed controls

Food-specific results

Russian salad: cases 10/16, controls 5/21, OR = 2.63
Tiramisu: cases 7/19, controls 3/23, OR = 2.82

Conclusion

Pasta with meat sauce has the highest odds ratio. It is the food most strongly associated with the outbreak and is the most likely vehicle of infection.
